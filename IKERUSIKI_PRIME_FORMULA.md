# IKERUSIKI Prime Formula

## Core Equation

S(n) = Survive(n; A_k) × AxisBirth(n)

## Purpose

This document defines the structural interpretation of prime emergence within IKERUSIKI Theory.

The objective is not to replace existing primality tests.

The objective is to study:

- Prime Axes
- Residual Space
- Mountain / Valley Structures
- Layered Positional Systems
## Definitions

### Prime Axis

A Prime Axis introduces a new elimination structure
that cannot be reproduced by combinations of previously
existing prime axes.
Examples:

- 2
- 3
- 5
- 7
- 11
- 13

Each prime introduces a new elimination structure into residue space.

### Residual Space

Residual Space is the set of positions that survive elimination by existing prime axes.

R_k = { r | gcd(r, B_k) = 1 }

where

B_k = 4 × ∏ p_i

### Mountain and Valley Structure

Mountain:
positions surviving elimination.

Valley:
positions eliminated by existing prime axes.

Prime candidates emerge only from surviving regions.
## Survive Operator

Survive(n; A_k)

represents survival against all elimination structures
generated by the existing prime-axis set A_k.

Survive(n; A_k) = 1

iff

n mod p ≠ 0

for all p ∈ A_k.
## AxisBirth Operator

AxisBirth(n)

represents the emergence of a new elimination structure.

A number becomes a new Prime Axis when its structural role
cannot be reproduced by combinations of previously existing
Prime Axes.

AxisBirth(n) = 1

iff

n introduces a new elimination structure
into Residual Space.

- survival against existing elimination structures
