# IKERUSIKI Experimental Models

## Purpose

This document describes experimental models used to investigate structural properties proposed in IKERUSIKI Theory.

The purpose is not to prove a theorem.

The purpose is to observe:

- Prime-axis emergence
- Residual-space structures
- Mountain / Valley formations
- Layered positional systems
- Candidate generation behavior

---

## Model 001

### Residual Space Observation

Objective:

Observe the structure that remains after elimination by existing prime axes.

Procedure:

1. Generate elimination structures from known prime axes.
2. Remove positions affected by those structures.
3. Observe surviving positions.
4. Record candidate emergence patterns.

Observation:

Surviving regions appear as structured residual spaces rather than random distributions.

## Model 002

### Layered Positional Analysis

Objective:

Procedure:

- Layer formation

- Positional persistence
